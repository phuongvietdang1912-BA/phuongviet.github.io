Customer Segmentation &
Revenue Analysis (SQL)

Used SQL to analyse customer behaviour, loyalty performance, and revenue drivers to improve targeting, retention, and profitability.

  • View Query
  • View Report

Business Context

Transaction and customer data contain valuable business signals, but without structured analysis it is difficult to identify which customer groups drive value and where inefficiencies exist.

Problem Statement

The business needed a clearer understanding of customer behaviour, loyalty patterns, and revenue contribution in order to improve retention strategy and marketing effectiveness.

Objective

Use SQL to explore customer and transaction data, identify high-value segments, and uncover insights that support better decision-making.

Approach

  • Customer segmentation and loyalty behaviour
  • Revenue contribution by group
  • Payment method patterns
  • Potential inefficiencies in discount-related strategies

Key Insights

  • Revenue is concentrated: A smaller group of customers contributes a disproportionate share of value.
  • Loyalty matters: Some customer groups show stronger repeat spending and higher business value.
  • Discount use is not always efficient: Some segments appear overexposed to discounts without equivalent profitability uplift.
  • Behavioural patterns support targeting: Payment and loyalty trends provide useful signals for segmentation.

Recommendations

  • Prioritise high-value segments for retention and personalised campaigns
  • Reduce inefficient discounting where profitability is weak
  • Use behavioural segments to guide marketing strategy
  • Monitor segment-level performance regularly through reporting

Business Impact

This analysis supports smarter customer targeting, better allocation of marketing effort, and stronger profitability by revealing where customer value is concentrated.

Tools Used

SQL, SQL Server / BigQuery, Data Analysis, Customer Segmentation
