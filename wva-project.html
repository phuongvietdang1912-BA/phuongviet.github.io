World Vision Australia

Donor Journey Optimisation

Redesigned donor journeys using behavioural segmentation to improve conversion speed, channel effectiveness, and fundraising efficiency.

  • View Full Report

Business Context

World Vision Australia’s Bounceback campaign is designed to re-engage inactive supporters and encourage renewed giving. However, the existing approach applies a uniform communication journey across all donors, regardless of behaviour, campaign type, response speed, or channel responsiveness.

Problem Statement

This one-size-fits-all approach creates delayed donations, unnecessary touchpoints, and inefficient use of communication channels. It also increases the risk of donor fatigue and reduces the ability to capture value from fast responders and high-value donors.

Objective

Identify behavioural differences across donors and campaigns, then recommend a more effective engagement strategy to improve time-to-donation, channel sequencing, engagement efficiency, and fundraising outcomes.

Approach

  • Time-to-donation segmentation
  • Channel responsiveness analysis
  • Donor value segmentation
  • Engagement decay analysis

1. Engagement & Journey Efficiency

Figure 1. Total Journeys and Engagement Rate % by Touchpoint and Campaign

Engagement is highest at Touch 1 and falls sharply by Touch 3, showing that later-stage touches are much less efficient.

Figure 14. Engagement Decay by Campaign and Channel

Decay is not uniform. Birthday and Christmas drop quickly, while Education remains flatter but weaker overall.

Insight: The current journey is too long for many donors. Most engagement value is captured early, while later touches add cost with weaker returns.

2. Donation Timing Patterns

Figure 2. Donation Speed by Campaign Type

Birthday campaigns convert fastest, Christmas has a longer response tail, and Education tends to convert early or not at all.

Figure 3. Time-to-Donation Distribution

The distribution shows clear timing variation, suggesting that donor journeys should be tailored to campaign behaviour.

Insight: Donation timing is not consistent across campaigns. A better strategy is to align journey length and messaging cadence to campaign-specific response patterns.

3. Channel Responsiveness

Figure 8. FY24 Conversion Rate by Channel and Touchpoint

Direct Mail is strongest at Touch 1, SMS performs best at Touch 2, and Email becomes more important later in the journey.

Figure 9. First Gift Touch Distribution by Trigger Channel

This reinforces the sequencing pattern: Direct Mail acts as an early trigger, SMS as a reminder, and Email as a nurture channel.

Insight: Channels perform at different speeds. The evidence supports a sequenced strategy rather than equal treatment of Direct Mail, SMS, and Email.

4. Donor Value Analysis

Figure 10. Total Donation Value by Campaign Type

Christmas generates the highest total donation value, followed by Birthday, while Education contributes the least.

Figure 11. Average Donation Value per Year by Campaign Type

Christmas also leads on average donation value, while Birthday remains stable and Education underperforms.

Insight: Donor value is highly concentrated. High-value donors should be protected and prioritised, while lower-value groups may need more cost-efficient journeys.

Key Findings

  • Timing varies by campaign: Birthday converts fastest, Christmas converts more gradually, and Education is early-or-never.
  • Channels operate at different speeds: Direct Mail triggers early response, SMS supports reminders, and Email nurtures later-stage conversions.
  • Value is concentrated: High-value donors drive disproportionate revenue, especially in Christmas campaigns.
  • Engagement decay is real: Performance falls sharply after 2–3 touches, meaning the current uniform journey is inefficient.

Recommendations

  • Optimise channel sequencing by campaign and donor segment
  • Use donor value tiers to prioritise higher-value supporters
  • Redesign journeys by conversion speed rather than using a fixed sequence for all donors
  • Introduce a decision dashboard to monitor value, timing, and channel response

Business Impact

This solution can reduce unnecessary touchpoints, accelerate donation timing, improve campaign cost efficiency, and support more personalised supporter engagement.
