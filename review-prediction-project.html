Predicting Property Review Scores
Using Machine Learning

Developed a predictive model using structured and unstructured data to estimate property review scores and support service improvement decisions.

  • View Full Report

Business Context

Customer review scores strongly influence performance on digital property platforms. Better understanding of review outcomes can support pricing decisions, service improvement, and overall customer experience.

Problem Statement

The business needed a more accurate way to estimate review outcomes and identify the factors that shape customer satisfaction.

Objective

Build a machine learning model that predicts property review scores and provides practical insights for pricing and service optimisation.

Approach

I combined structured features and text-based information to compare several modelling approaches. The final solution used an ensemble method to improve prediction quality and reduce estimation error.

Key Insights

  • Combining structured and unstructured data improves performance
  • Review outcomes are influenced by multiple operational factors, not a single variable
  • Ensemble modelling delivered stronger accuracy than simpler standalone approaches
  • Predictive outputs can support practical decisions in pricing and service design

Recommendations

  • Use predicted review patterns to identify properties at risk of lower customer satisfaction
  • Incorporate review drivers into service improvement planning
  • Use both quantitative and text data more consistently in future models
  • Support pricing decisions with model-based satisfaction insights

Business Impact

This project improved predictive accuracy and created a stronger analytical basis for using customer feedback data in pricing, service, and operational decisions.

Tools Used

Rapidminer, Model Random Forest, Model Gradient Boosted Trees, Model Decision Tree.
